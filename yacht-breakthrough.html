In the long and storied history of superyacht construction, certain vessels mark not merely an advancement in scale or luxury but a genuine turning point in maritime technology. BREAKTHROUGH — Feadship's Project 821 — is one such vessel. Delivered in May 2025, the 118.8-metre motor yacht represents nothing less than the dawn of a new era: she is the world's first superyacht to harness hydrogen fuel cell technology as a primary power source, capable of operating with zero diesel emissions over meaningful distances at sea.

Commissioned by Patrick Dovigi, the Canadian entrepreneur and founder of waste management firm GFL Environmental, BREAKTHROUGH was built to a brief that demanded the greenest, most environmentally advanced yacht ever constructed — without compromise on luxury or capability. Feadship, the prestigious Dutch cooperative formed by Royal Van Lent Shipyard and Koninklijke De Vries Scheepsbouw, answered that challenge with a vessel that pushes the frontier of what is technically achievable on the water.

The propulsion system at the heart of BREAKTHROUGH is unprecedented in scale. Sixteen PowerCell H2 fuel cells rated at 185 kW each provide clean electrical power, drawing on cryogenic liquid hydrogen stored aboard at an extraordinary minus 253 degrees Celsius — just twenty degrees above absolute zero. This hydrogen reacts electrochemically with oxygen, producing electrical current with water as the sole byproduct. Complementing the fuel cell array are three 2,500 kW MTU generators and two 900 kW MTU generators, ensuring full capability when operating in conventional mode. Two 3,200 kW ABB azimuthing pod drives translate that power into propulsion, delivering a maximum speed of 17 knots and a range of 6,500 nautical miles at 14 knots.

The engineering achievement required years of regulatory development — Feadship worked closely with classification society Lloyd's Register and the flag state to establish new rules governing hydrogen storage and fuel cell systems at class, flag-state, and IMO level. The result is a vessel that is simultaneously a proof of concept and a fully operable superyacht of the highest order.

Exterior and interior design were entrusted to British studio RWD (Redman Whiteley Dixon), who crafted a silhouette of understated authority. Five decks of accommodation serve up to 30 guests in exceptional comfort, attended by a crew of 44. Naval architecture was managed by De Voogt Naval Architects, Feadship's in-house design bureau. The vessel displaces 7,247 GT, making her one of the most voluminous private yachts in existence, and her 19-metre beam provides exceptional interior volume across all decks.

In the months following delivery, BREAKTHROUGH was spotted in Antibes — a sighting that generated considerable attention in the yachting world — and later sighted in the Caribbean. Her presence at any anchorage commands immediate recognition: the scale, the clean lines, and the knowledge that beneath her hull lies a technology that may define superyacht propulsion for the next generation. BREAKTHROUGH is for sale at an asking price reported in the region of $645 million, making her one of the most valuable private vessels ever offered to the market. Whether she changes hands or remains with her owner, she has already changed the industry.
