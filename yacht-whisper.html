WHISPER carries with her a history that spans two ownerships, two names, and a decade of high-profile presence in global superyacht circles. Built in 2014 by Krögerwerft at Schacht-Audorf in Germany — a yard closely associated with Lürssen — she was delivered to Pakistani-American billionaire Shahid Khan as KISMET, a name that would be transferred to her considerably larger successor when the original was sold in late 2023. Her new owner, former Google CEO Eric Schmidt, renamed her WHISPER upon acquisition.

Exterior design is the work of Espen Oeino International, the Monaco-based studio whose portfolio spans some of the most recognisable superyachts of the past two decades. Oeino's signature approach — clean, aggressive profiles with purposeful detailing — is evident in WHISPER's silhouette: a vessel that manages to look fast even at rest. Interior design responsibility was assigned to Reymond Langton Design, the London studio whose aesthetic sensibility leans toward sophisticated restraint, deploying fine materials and considered proportions rather than spectacle.

At 95.2 metres, WHISPER occupies an interesting position in the superyacht market: substantial enough to command genuine authority at any anchorage, yet nimble by the standards of the largest vessels. Her gross tonnage of 2,928 GT and beam of 13.8 metres provide generous interior volume across multiple decks, accommodating up to 16 guests in comfort and style.

Under her original name, the vessel was managed by Moran Yacht & Ship and was a frequent participant in the global charter circuit, spending summers in the Mediterranean and winters in the Caribbean. She was photographed at numerous marquee locations — Canary Wharf during the London 2012 period, the French Riviera in season, and a range of eastern Mediterranean destinations. Her presence at the Sète anchorage in France was captured in photographs that remain among the most widely reproduced images of the vessel.

Under Eric Schmidt's ownership, WHISPER continues to operate actively, her AIS transponder registering movements consistent with a vessel in regular private use. She stands as an elegant example of Lürssen-affiliated construction from the mid-2010s — a period that produced some of the most refined superyachts of the modern era — and her continued active operation is testament to the quality of her original build and subsequent maintenance.
