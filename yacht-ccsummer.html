CC SUMMER — formerly known as Madsummer — is a 95-metre motor yacht built by Lürssen at their German facilities and launched in March 2019. One of the yard's most accomplished deliveries of that year, she represents Lürssen's characteristic approach to vessels in the 90-100 metre range: technically sophisticated, built to exceptional quality standards, and capable of sustained ocean passage without compromise.

Lürssen, based in Bremen with facilities at Lemwerder and Rendsburg, has been building vessels for over 140 years and has established itself as the preeminent builder of the world's largest private yachts. The construction of CC SUMMER — then Madsummer — drew on all of this accumulated expertise. At 95 metres and 14 metres beam, the vessel occupies a scale at which Lürssen delivers their full range of engineering capability: advanced stabilisation, integrated management systems, and the material quality that justifies the yard's reputation and the prices it commands.

Following her delivery in 2019 under the name Madsummer, the vessel appears to have changed ownership and been renamed CC SUMMER at some point in her operational life — a relatively common occurrence in the superyacht world, where vessels frequently change hands within the first few years of operation and new owners typically wish to assert their own identity. The Marshall Islands flag under which she now operates — MMSI 538071410, callsign V7A2434 — is one of the most widely used registries for private yachts, offering a recognised and efficient flag state administration from an internationally respected registry.

A beam-to-length ratio in this range typically delivers wide salon proportions, comfortable guest staterooms, and substantial exterior deck areas suited to warm-weather cruising.
