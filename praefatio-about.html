In preparation

What we are doing.

A correspondence project. Two students. Fifty letters. One question each.

Over the next eighteen months we will write fifty letters — some hand-typed and posted, some sent by email — to founders, investors, and operators we admire. Each is preceded by hours of reading: every long-form profile, every annual letter, every obscure interview we can find. We are not journalists. We are not selling anything. We ask a single question, shaped by a turning point in the recipient's life, and wait to see what comes back.

The replies, with permission, become a publication. The first edition collects thirty letters. Half from people who built companies worth a billion or more. Half from people we suspect will, but have not yet.

Most of what the next generation learns about business is filtered through podcasts, ghostwritten memoirs and quotes recycled until they mean nothing. We are trying something older. — The Editors

Why letters.

The work is patient on purpose. Most of what gets written today exists to be scrolled past. We are interested in what survives — what someone says when they have time to consider the answer, and a reason to commit it to paper.

If you are a founder, an investor, an operator, or any other kind of builder — and someone you've never heard of writes you a letter that begins with this name on the masthead — that is us. Read it.

What you can expect.

One question. Ten minutes of your time. A stamped envelope, or a reply-to address that goes only to the two of us. Anything you write back will be treated with the respect we hope it deserves: nothing published without permission, nothing quoted out of context, nothing repurposed.
