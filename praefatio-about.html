The Praefatio
Letters to the people who built the world.

Two students writing - by post and by email - to the founders, investors and operators they admire. One question each, shaped by hours of reading.

What we are doing.

A prologue to a journey of creation and start-ups. We are set out not only to research, but to understand, those who have built the world.
JT & RP

The plan is fifty letters in the next eighteen months. Some of them typed on paper and posted with a real stamp, others sent by email. They go out to founders, investors, and people who have built the kind of companies we read about. Before any letter gets written we spend hours reading whatever we can find on the person: profiles, old interviews, annual letters, anything long enough to actually say something.

We're not journalists, and we are not trying to sell anything. We ask one question, and it's usually about a moment in their life where they changed their mind, or had to make a hard call. Then we wait and see if they write back.

If they let us, we publish the reply. The first issue is thirty exchanges. Roughly half from people who have already built billion-dollar companies, and half from people who haven't yet, but we think will.

Most of what our generation learns about starting a company comes from a broken school system, basic questions, podcasts and meaningless quotes. We are going deeper into the minds and thought processes of the world's founders. The Editors

Why letters.

Most of what gets written online is built to be skimmed and forgotten. We wanted the opposite. A letter slows everything down, both for us and for the person reading it. You think harder when you have to write it out, and people answer differently when they know the question wasn't fired off in thirty seconds.

If a letter shows up on your desk one day from two names you have never heard of, with this masthead at the top, that's probably us. Please read it.

What you can expect.

One question. About ten minutes of your time. A stamped envelope back to us, or a hand-typed email that only the two of us ever read.

If you do write back, here is what we promise:

  • nothing gets published unless you say yes
  • nothing gets pulled out of context to make a clip
  • nothing gets reused for anything else, ever

The whole point of this is to keep what you say intact, not to chop it up.
