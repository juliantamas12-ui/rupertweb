LEVIATHAN is the most personal superyacht in the modern era — a vessel built not to impress but to function, conceived from first principles through an unprecedented collaboration between owner, future crew, and builder. Owned by Gabe Newell, the American video game developer, Valve Corporation co-founder, and, since 2024, the owner of the Oceanco shipyard itself, she represents a philosophical statement about how superyachts should be designed and what they should ultimately achieve.

Oceanco, the Dutch yard with a history of ambitious projects including Jubilee, Bravo Eugenia, and Black Pearl, delivered LEVIATHAN in November 2025 following sea trials off the coast of Stellendam, the Netherlands. At 111 metres in length with a beam of 17.8 metres, she is the third-largest yacht Oceanco has ever built. Her gross tonnage and interior volume place her firmly in the tier occupied by only the most significant private vessels.

What distinguishes LEVIATHAN from comparable vessels is her design philosophy. From the outset, Newell insisted that the yacht be conceived collaboratively — involving not just the owner's representatives but the crew who would actually operate her on a daily basis. The result is a vessel where operational efficiency, crew wellbeing, and habitability are treated as primary design parameters rather than afterthoughts. Oceanco described the finished vessel as "the most comfortable yacht ever designed, built and delivered" by the yard — a claim supported by her sea trial results.

Exterior design is the work of Oceanco's own design team, producing a profile that has been described as striking in its deliberate unconventionality. The vessel's finish, reported to deploy an extensive palette of grey tones — earning early media coverage referring to her aesthetic — creates an impression of mass and authority that is quite different from the polished white or gleaming dark hulls typical of vessels in this size class. Interior design by Mark Berryman Design brings the same human-centric philosophy into the domestic spaces.

The vessel features a submarine garage — capable of housing and deploying a personal submersible — a dedicated hospital facility, spa, and gaming computers. These features, unusual even by superyacht standards, reflect Newell's background and priorities as a technologist and his stated commitment to crew welfare. Her names inscription of all 2,000+ contributors on a glass panel in the main staircase stands as an unusual gesture of collective recognition in an industry that often treats labour as invisible. LEVIATHAN is not merely a superyacht; she is an argument about what superyachts could be.
