The Editors
01 — The Project

What we are doing.

The Praefatio is a correspondence project. Over the next eighteen months, we will write fifty hand-typed letters to founders, investors, and operators we admire. Each letter is preceded by hours of reading: every long-form profile, every annual letter, every obscure interview we can find. We are not journalists. We are not selling anything. We are asking each recipient a single deeply-researched question about a turning point in their life — and waiting to see what comes back.

The replies, with permission, become a publication. The first edition collects thirty letters. Half from people who built companies worth a billion or more. Half from people we suspect will, but have not yet.

The work is patient on purpose. Most of what gets written today exists to be scrolled past. We are interested in what survives — what someone says when they have time to consider the answer, and a reason to commit it to paper.

02 — Methodology

How we work.

i.

Two to three hours of research per letter.

We read everything publicly available about the subject before writing a single word. The question we ask must reflect that reading.

ii.

One question per letter — based on a real pivot.

Each letter asks a single question about a turning point in the subject's life: a moment they changed direction, contradicted an earlier position, or made a choice they may not have explained publicly. We do not send surveys.

iii.

Paper, not pixels.

Letters are printed, hand-signed, and sent by post with a stamped reply envelope. The medium is the proof of seriousness. Almost no one writes letters anymore. The ones who reply do so because of that.

iv.

Nothing is published without permission.

Every letter we send includes explicit language about how a reply might be used. Anyone may decline publication, restrict use, or speak entirely off the record. We honour every instruction in writing.

04 — Specimen

A draft letter.

An example of what we send. This particular letter is in preparation; it has not yet been posted.

Draft
From The Praefatio · Rome · MMXXVI
Dear Mr Collison,

We are Julian Tamas and Rocco. We are fourteen, students at Marymount International School in Rome, and we are writing to thirty founders whose work we have spent the past year reading.

Before this letter, we read your sister's profile of you in The New Yorker, your essays at patrickcollison.com, every Stripe shareholder letter, and the early Lisp interpreter you wrote at fourteen. What struck us most was a single thing: you were a published research scientist in your teens, and then you put science down and built Stripe.

You left research science behind to build a payments company. Twenty years later, do you think of yourself as someone who abandoned science, or someone who applied it to something different — and what would you tell a fourteen-year-old facing a similar choice today?

That is the only question. We have enclosed a stamped envelope for your reply. If you would prefer to write off the record, or not at all, we will respect either entirely.

— Julian & Rocco
