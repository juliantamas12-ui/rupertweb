The Mission

We write letters that get answered.

Most of what the next generation learns about business is filtered through podcasts, ghostwritten memoirs, and quotes recycled until they mean nothing. We are trying something older. We write, by hand, to the people we want to learn from — and we ask the questions a chatbot cannot.

The Praefatio is a research project run by two students. Each letter is preceded by hours of reading: every long-form profile, every annual letter, every obscure interview from before the subject was famous. We are not journalists. We are not consultants. We are not asking for anything but ten minutes of honesty.

The output is a book, a quarterly publication, and a small archive that will outlast both. The first edition collects thirty letters. Half from people who built companies worth a billion or more. Half from people we suspect will, but have not yet.

The work is patient on purpose. Most of what gets written today exists to be scrolled past. We are interested in what survives — what someone says when they have time to consider the answer, and a reason to commit it to paper.

If you are a founder, an investor, an operator, or any other kind of builder — and someone you've never heard of writes you a letter that begins with this name on the masthead — that is us. Read it.

The Method

Three principles. Held strictly.

No survey. No automated outreach. No clever growth tricks. The method is the moat.

i

Research, not interrogation.

Two to three hours of preparation precede every letter. We arrive having read what the subject already said. We ask only what they have not been asked.

ii

Three questions, no more.

Each letter contains a single hook, a single proof of work, and three questions. One forces opinion. One forces specificity. One forces regret. Ten minutes of their time. No survey.

iii

Paper, not pixels.

The letters are printed and posted. Hand-signed. Stamped envelope returned for the reply. The medium is the proof of seriousness. Almost no one writes letters anymore.

"What survives is what someone took the time to write down."
— The Editors, 2026
