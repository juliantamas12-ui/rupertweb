What we are doing We write letters that get answered.

Over the next eighteen months we will write fifty hand-typed letters to founders, investors, and operators we admire. Each letter is preceded by hours of reading: every long-form profile, every annual letter, every obscure interview we can find. We are not journalists. We are not selling anything. We ask a single question — one shaped by a turning point in the recipient's life — and wait to see what comes back.

The replies, with permission, become a publication. The first edition collects thirty letters. Half from people who built companies worth a billion or more. Half from people we suspect will, but have not yet.

The work is patient on purpose. Most of what gets written today exists to be scrolled past. We are interested in what survives.

Most of what the next generation learns is filtered through podcasts, ghostwritten memoirs and quotes recycled until they mean nothing. We are trying something older.

— The Editors

Methodology

Four rules we don't break.

The method is the moat. No surveys, no automated outreach, no clever growth tricks.

i.

Two to three hours of research per letter.

We read everything publicly available about the subject before writing a single word. The question we ask must reflect that reading.

ii.

One question per letter — about a real pivot.

A turning point in the subject's life: a moment they changed direction, contradicted an earlier position, or made a choice they may not have explained publicly.

iii.

Paper, not pixels.

Letters are printed, hand-signed, and posted with a stamped reply envelope. The medium is the proof of seriousness.

iv.

Nothing is published without permission.

Every letter includes explicit language about how a reply might be used. Anyone may decline, restrict, or speak off the record. We honour every instruction.
