Sample · Letter draft № 03 · 2026-05
Dear Mr Marks,

We are Julian and Rocco — fourteen years old, students in Rome. We've read every Oaktree memo since 1990. You've written that the most important thing is "second-level thinking," but we've also seen you write, in 2007, that you weren't worried enough about leverage.

Three questions, ten minutes, a stamped envelope enclosed:

— What did you believe in 1990 that you no longer believe?
— What is the price of being a contrarian who turns out to be wrong?
— What should we, at fourteen, do that you wish someone had told you?

We have read fifty of your memos. We have no agenda. We have an envelope.

— J & R
