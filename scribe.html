02 · QUESTION ARCHITECTURE

The Anatomy of a Letter.

Same skeleton every time. Three questions max. Each must be unanswerable by ChatGPT.

The Five Beats

  1. Hook (2 lines) — who we are, why this letter exists.
  2. Proof of research (3-5 lines) — quote one obscure thing they said.
  3. Three questions — opinion / contradiction / regret.
  4. The output — what we'll do with their answer.
  5. Permission to ignore — close graciously.

Master Questions

Stock pool. Pick one or adapt for each target.

"What's one thing you believed at 25 you now think is completely wrong?"
"Name a decision between 14 & 18 that you can directly trace to where you are now."
"What's the most expensive lesson you've learned that nobody warned you about?"
"If you could only teach a 14-year-old one mental model, which one — and why?"
03 · TEMPLATE

The Letter.

Print on Crane's stationery, hand-sign in fountain pen, post to office address with SASE enclosed.

Dear Mr Smith,

We are Julian Tamas and [friend]. We are fourteen, we are at school in Rome, and we are writing the same letter to fifty founders, investors and operators we admire.

We chose you because of [one specific, obscure thing — quote them, cite a year, reference a deal/decision they rarely talk about]. Reading that helped us understand [what we took from it].

If you have ten minutes, we'd like to ask three questions:

1. Question one — opinion-forcing, requires personal reflection.

2. Question two — references something specific from their career.

3. Question three — a regret, contradiction, or "what I would do differently".

Your answer will be one chapter in a book we are compiling for under-eighteens. You will receive a bound copy when published, and any net royalties will be donated to [a real charity, name it].

We have enclosed a stamped reply envelope. We understand you may not have the time, and we are grateful regardless.

Yours sincerely,

Julian Tamas & [Friend]
Rome, Italy · julian@scribe-letters.com

04 · RESEARCH WORKFLOW

Two to Three Hours Per Target.

No exceptions. The depth shows in the letter and is the entire reason they reply.

  1. Wikipedia + 3 best long-form profiles.   The New Yorker, FT Lunch With, Bloomberg Businessweek, Forbes profile.
  2. Their podcast appearances.   YouTube → search transcripts. Skim the longest one (2h+) at 1.5×.
  3. Books and annual letters.   Buffett's letters, Howard Marks's memos, anyone's published TED talk.
  4. Search "[name] regret" "[name] mistake" "[name] would do differently".   The gold lives here.
  5. Find the one obscure 2007–2014 interview   where they said something they wouldn't say today. That becomes Question 2.
  6. Draft three questions.   Each unanswerable by AI. Each forces opinion.
05 · IDEAS

The Notebook.

Anything you and your friend think of — questions, targets, channel ideas, doubts. Saved on this device.

    Reminders & Principles

    • One ask per letter. Never ask for an interview AND advice AND a meeting.
    • Always include SASE for top-tier targets. £1 extra, 3× reply rate.
    • Track every send + response in the Targets section. Don't double-send.
    • If a target replies, send a thank-you within 24h regardless of usefulness.
    • Build the audience before asking the biggest names. 5–10 yes responses first.
    • Get every reply with a signed release form before quoting in the book.
