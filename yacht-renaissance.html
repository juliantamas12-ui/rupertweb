112m — Freire Shipyard — 2023

About This Vessel

RENAISSANCE represents one of the most remarkable superyacht projects of 2023 and a landmark achievement for Freire Shipyard, the historic Spanish yard based in Vigo, Galicia. At 112 metres in length, she is Spain's largest ever superyacht, and her classification as an expedition-capable luxury vessel places her in a highly sought-after category: the explorer yacht capable of reaching the world's most remote destinations without sacrificing the comfort and amenity expected by her guests.

Freire Shipyard has been building oceangoing vessels for over a century, but RENAISSANCE represents their most ambitious superyacht programme to date. The construction process involved the full mobilisation of the yard's engineering and fabrication capabilities, with the vessel's steel hull and aluminium superstructure built to the demanding standards required for extended ocean passages in challenging conditions. Her structural scantlings and systems redundancy reflect genuine expedition capability rather than the cosmetic "explorer" aesthetic adopted by some contemporaries.

Available for charter through YachtCharterFleet and associated brokerages, RENAISSANCE can accommodate up to 36 guests across 19 cabins — an exceptional guest capacity for a vessel of her type. She carries a crew of 45, ensuring that service levels are maintained even at full charter capacity. The ratio of crew to guests reflects the owner's and charterer's expectation of hotel-standard attention throughout the voyage.

Her expedition credentials are genuine. RENAISSANCE's extended range and robust systems allow her to operate in polar regions, cross major oceans independently, and sustain operations far from port infrastructure. This capability, combined with her considerable luxury appointments, positions her as one of the most versatile charter assets in the global fleet — equally suited to a summer season in the Mediterranean and a winter Antarctic expedition.

Since her delivery in 2023, RENAISSANCE has generated significant interest among the charter market, with her combination of scale, expedition capability, and Spanish build quality representing a compelling proposition. Her IMO number 9880776 and MMSI 319268600 confirm her active AIS status, and she continues to operate under the Cayman Islands flag. She stands as compelling evidence that the superyacht building industry is no longer the exclusive domain of the Netherlands, Germany, and Italy.
