In preparation

Four rules we don't break.

The method is the moat. No surveys. No automated outreach. No clever growth tricks. The same discipline that makes a letter worth answering is what makes the project worth doing.

i.

Two to three hours of research per letter.

We read everything publicly available about the subject before writing a single word: every long-form profile, every annual letter, every interview from before they were famous. The question we ask must reflect that reading. If a chatbot could have answered it, we have not asked it yet.

ii.

One question per letter — about a real pivot.

A turning point in the subject's life: a moment they changed direction, contradicted an earlier position, or made a choice they may not have explained publicly. The question is short, specific, and unanswerable without consideration. We do not send surveys, we do not ask for a list of advice.

iii.

By post, or by email.

When we believe the medium matters, we print, hand-sign, and post with a stamped reply envelope. When the recipient prefers digital, we write a properly composed email and send it from hello@thepraefatio.com. The form follows the reader, not the other way around. Almost no one writes letters anymore. The ones who reply often do so because of that.

iv.

Nothing is published without permission.

Every letter includes explicit language about how a reply might be used. Anyone may decline publication, restrict use to specific contexts, or speak entirely off the record. We honour every instruction in writing and confirm in advance of publication. The trust on which this project depends is built one reply at a time.
