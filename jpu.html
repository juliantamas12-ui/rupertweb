The University

A small university with an unusual address.

Twelve hundred students, eight senior fellows, and a single coastline at the Pacific's most biodiverse shore. Juan Pelotes is not a tourist destination. It is one of the most selective independent research universities in the Southern Hemisphere.

Headmaster of the University

Joe Jonas Sterling

Ph.D. Evolutionary Systems · Appointed 2026

Sterling assumed the headmastership of Juan Pelotes in 2026, following a distinguished decade in comparative biology at institutions in Quito and Oxford. His appointment continues the university's tradition of installing academics first and administrators second.

His stated vision: to maintain JPU as a small institution that behaves like a small institution — a rare thing in modern higher education. Twelve hundred students. Long commitments. Sustained fieldwork. No shortcuts to the degree.

"Prestige is not reputation. Reputation is what others decide about you. Prestige is what you actually do when nobody is looking. Our job is the second."
Department of Drug Inspection & Biology
Chief Executive · Inspection

Bob Timothy IV

M.D., Biosecurity Systems · Appointed 2018

Bob Timothy IV directs the university's Department of Drug Inspection & Biology — a division established at the university's founding to ensure nothing arrives at, and nothing departs from, Isabela without documented biological and pharmaceutical review.

Timothy reports independently to the University Council and the Ecuadorian Ministry of Environment. His department holds full autonomous authority: no shipment, sample, or specimen passes the island boundary without clearance from his office.

"If I do my work well, you will never hear my name again. If you hear my name again, something has arrived here that should not have."
Academic Departments

Five departments. One coastline.

Each department is led by a single senior fellow, and each operates under the university's Net-Positive mandate: research must demonstrably improve the biological integrity of the Archipelago.

I.

Regenerative Ecology

The foundational department. Invasive species management, endemic taxonomy, tortoise repopulation, and the ongoing revision of the Galápagos Species Index maintained by the university.

II.

Maritime Engineering

Sustainable naval architecture and the engineering of coastal life-support systems. Our submersible fleet departs from the university pier and returns to the same.

III.

Climate Geopolitics

Environmental law, international treaty analysis, and the jurisprudence of extraterritorial scientific enclaves. The smallest department; the most published.

IV.

Evolutionary Computation

The application of biological evolutionary principles to adaptive systems and machine learning architecture. Partnered with three European safety institutes.

V. — Special Charter

Department of Drug Inspection & Biology

Operated under a charter independent of the academic college. The department conducts all pharmaceutical oversight, biological screening, import/export manifest review, quarantine enforcement, and the chain-of-custody documentation required for every molecule, sample, and specimen crossing the Isabela boundary. Reports jointly to the University Council and to the Ministry of Environment of Ecuador. Directed by Bob Timothy IV.

The Isabela Campus

A university built on the shore.

Isabela Island · West Coast
−0.50° S · −91.10° W

On the Pacific shoreline.

The Juan Pelotes campus sits directly on the western coast of Isabela Island, a half-hour's walk from Puerto Villamil. Every academic building opens to the ocean. The marine research pier is the same pier that supplies the faculty of Maritime Engineering. The library faces south.

A measured footprint.

Enrolment is capped at 1,200 students as a matter of founding charter — not a matter of capacity. The university does not grow. It sustains.

The No-Trace Covenant.

Every student, fellow, and visiting researcher is bound by the No-Trace Covenant: no synthetic material may leave campus without documented review. No biological sample departs the archipelago without approval from the Department of Drug Inspection & Biology. The university must demonstrably leave the island in better biological health than it found it. Every admission decision is measured against that single clause.

Residency Required

Juan Pelotes admits approximately seventy students each year across five departments. The admit rate has not risen above seven per cent since 2009. The university conducts a single admissions intake per calendar year.

There is no early decision, no rolling admission, and no mechanism by which a student may transfer in after the first year. Residency on Isabela for the full four years of study is non-negotiable, as is the Department of Drug Inspection & Biology's review of every incoming student's medical and pharmaceutical manifest.

Applicants are assessed against one question above all others: can this person carry the No-Trace Covenant for four years without supervision?
