02 / The Isabela Campus

Built into the shield of the volcano.

LAT −0.5° · LONG −91.1°
JPU — Zone I

Topography as architecture.

The JPU campus occupies 1,400 hectares along the western slope of Isabela Island, embedded into the basalt of the Sierra Negra shield system. There are no imported materials in the residential grid. There is no imported light at night. Buildings are carved into the geology they sit on.

Operated off-grid.

A decentralised hydrogen fuel-cell grid, powered by solar electrolysis on the southern plateau, supplies all energy requirements. The university exports surplus to the local community under bilateral agreement with the provincial government. The institution draws zero power from Ecuador's national network.

A closed human footprint.

Isabela hosts fewer than 3,000 permanent residents. The university's presence was negotiated through a 99-year lease conditioned on one inviolable principle: no net ecological impact. The university must demonstrably leave the island in better biological health than it found it. Every structure, every policy, every admission decision is measured against that clause.

◆ The No-Trace Policy

All students, faculty, and visiting researchers are bound by the No-Trace Covenant: no synthetic material may leave the campus perimeter without Inspection Division documentation. No biological sample may exit the archipelago without Ministry review. Bicycles only beyond Gate 2. Violation results in immediate revocation of residency.

03 / Research

Four disciplines, one boundary.

Every project must demonstrate either direct benefit to the Galápagos ecosystem or indirect benefit through applied scientific output. This is the institutional Net-Positive mandate.

01 / REGENERATIVE ECOLOGY

Endemic Biology & Restoration

The largest college. Focus areas: invasive species management, tortoise repopulation programmes, and the taxonomy of previously undocumented deep-sea species near Isabela's submerged calderas.

02 / MARITIME ENGINEERING

Closed-Circuit Systems

Sustainable naval architecture, ocean-current energy capture, pressurised research submersibles, and the engineering of life-support systems for the Sub-Oceanic Laboratory.

03 / CLIMATE GEOPOLITICS

Policy & Jurisdiction

An interdisciplinary faculty combining environmental law, international treaty analysis, and the legal frameworks governing extraterritorial scientific enclaves.

04 / EVOLUTIONARY AI

Adaptive Systems

Applies evolutionary biological principles to machine learning architecture. The youngest college. Partnered with three European AI safety institutes.
