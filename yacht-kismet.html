KISMET is the second superyacht to bear that name for her owner, Pakistani-American billionaire Shahid Khan — owner of Fulham Football Club and the Jacksonville Jaguars. Where her predecessor, the 95-metre Kismet (now renamed Whisper following its sale to Eric Schmidt), represented a benchmark of its era, the 2024 KISMET is an altogether grander statement: at 122 metres overall length, she ranks among the twenty largest private motor yachts in the world.

Built at Lürssen's Lemwerder facility in Germany and delivered in late 2024 following sea trials, she was formerly known during construction as Project Jag — a reference, presumably, to the leaping jaguar figurehead mounted on her bow, a bold and theatrical touch that gives her profile an immediately recognisable character. That motif, rendered in polished metal, serves as both decoration and declaration: this is a vessel built to be noticed.

Exterior design responsibility fell to Nuvolari Lenard, the respected Italian studio responsible for a distinguished portfolio of superyacht profiles. The result is a sleek, elongated silhouette with flowing lines and generous deck overhangs that balance presence with speed. Interior design was entrusted to the London firm Reymond Langton Design — the same studio responsible for the interior of the original Kismet — ensuring a consistency of aesthetic vision across both vessels, though at considerably elevated scale and ambition.

KISMET made her public debut at the Monaco Yacht Show in September 2024, where interior photographs released by Lürssen revealed spaces of exceptional craftsmanship. The salon, dining room, and owner's suite all demonstrated the hallmarks of Reymond Langton's approach: richly textured materials, considered lighting design, and a palette that manages to feel simultaneously contemporary and timeless.

She is listed for charter through Cecil Wright, one of the most established brokerages in the superyacht market, with a weekly charter rate placing her firmly at the top of the global charter market. Her guest capacity and crew complement — typical of a vessel of her class — ensure that large private parties can be accommodated in complete comfort and privacy. KISMET represents Lürssen and her owner at their most ambitious, a vessel that will define both legacies for decades to come.
