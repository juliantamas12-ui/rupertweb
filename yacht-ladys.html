LADY S is a 93-metre motor yacht delivered by Feadship in 2019, representing one of the finest examples of Dutch custom superyacht construction to emerge in the latter part of that decade. Registered under the Cayman Islands flag with MMSI 319137200, she operates at the upper tier of private yachting, combining the technical excellence for which Feadship is renowned with an interior concept of understated luxury and refined maritime elegance.

The exterior lines of LADY S were drawn by Michael Leach Design, a studio known for producing profiles that age well — neither aggressively contemporary nor tied to any particular design moment. The result is a silhouette of balanced proportions, with a broad beam of 14 metres delivering considerable interior volume across her multiple decks. Interior design was handled by the acclaimed London studio Reymond Langton Design, who applied their characteristic approach of muted tones and tailored detail to create spaces that feel genuinely residential rather than merely luxurious.

Feadship's construction quality is self-evident throughout the vessel. The yard's commitment to bespoke engineering means that no two Feadship vessels share more than the broadest design language; every aspect of LADY S was developed to the specific requirements of her owner. The hull is steel, the superstructure aluminium, and the deck surfaces finished in teak — conventional materials deployed with exceptional precision.

Among the notable features reported aboard are generous exterior deck spaces across multiple levels, a swimming pool, beach club with direct sea access, and a suite of water toys typical of a vessel of her class. The main salon, owner's suite, and guest accommodation reflect Reymond Langton's careful material selection, with custom joinery, curated art placement, and lighting that transitions naturally between day and evening atmospheres.

Since her delivery, LADY S has been documented in various Mediterranean locations, including a notable recent AIS position placing her in the Barcelona area. Her active AIS transponder and frequent movements suggest a vessel that is regularly used rather than laid up — a hallmark of an owner who takes genuine pleasure in time at sea. She represents Feadship at its most considered: not the largest yacht in the fleet, but one where every design decision has been made with care and executed without compromise.
