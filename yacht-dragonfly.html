DRAGONFLY is one of the most significant private vessels launched in the twenty-first century. At 142.08 metres, she is the 13th-longest motor yacht in the world, and her delivery by Lürssen in late 2024 marked a defining moment for both the German shipyard and for the private yachting world at large. Owned by Google co-founder Sergey Brin, she was built under the project code "Alibaba" — a name that generated considerable speculation during the years of her secretive construction before the vessel's true identity was confirmed.

The commission is notable for numerous reasons. Brin, despite being one of the wealthiest individuals on the planet, has historically maintained an unusually low public profile in matters of personal expenditure. DRAGONFLY's scale — her gross tonnage of 9,408 GT makes her one of the most voluminous private yachts ever built — represents a significant departure from that discretion. Her beam of 20 metres provides interior space that rivals a small cruise ship, while her propulsion system — twin MAN diesels producing 9,785 horsepower each — delivers a maximum speed of 24 knots, an exceptional figure for a vessel of this size.

Exterior design was the work of German Frers, the Argentine-Italian studio known for producing profiles that combine classical maritime authority with modern refinement. The result is a vessel of forceful elegance, characterised by crisp profile lines, suspended aft decks, and vast open exterior spaces. Interior design responsibility fell to Nauta Design, a studio with a distinguished track record in large yacht interiors, who were responsible for creating spaces of appropriate grandeur without sacrificing the livability essential in a vessel intended for genuine use.

Construction at Lürssen's Rendsburg facility began in the early 2020s, with the keel laid in conditions of considerable secrecy. The vessel was launched in December 2023 and completed sea trials in the North Sea near Brunsbüttel in September 2024. Delivery to her owner occurred in November 2024, following which she was registered under the Cayman Islands flag with callsign ZGSU7.

Since delivery, DRAGONFLY has been documented at multiple locations including Puerto Rico, where she was observed at the Safe Harbor San Juan marina. She accommodates up to 24 guests and operates with a crew of 53 — figures consistent with her ambition and scale. In a fleet already populated by extraordinary vessels, DRAGONFLY stands apart: the largest, the fastest at her size, and the most technologically advanced vessel Lürssen has delivered in the current decade.
